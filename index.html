What is a doula?

A doula is a professional who is trained to provide support for families before, after, and during birth

Doulas provide emotional support, give information to families regarding the birth and their choices surrounding the birth, and utilize their training to provide comfort measures as well as encouragement.

Doulas do not make choices for families. They are not medical professionals and will not give diagnoses, exams, or medical advice. However, they may provide you with literature that can help you make decisions. A true doula does not judge, and does not let their personal biases interfere in any way with your birth experience. They are simply their to support families in their decisions.

Finding about doula support

For most of these outcomes*, the best results occurred when woman had continuous labor support from a doula– someone who was NOT a staff member at the hospital and who was NOT part of the woman’s social network.

For four of these outcomes,* results with a doula were better than all the other types of continuous support that were studied. For the other outcomes, there was no difference between types of continuous support. (2012, Hodnett et al.) Please read further for more information about doula support at Evidence Based Birth.

    When continuous labor support was provided by a doula, women experienced a:

    31% decrease in the use of Pitocin*

    28% decrease in the risk of C-section*

    12% increase in the likelihood of a spontaneous vaginal birth*

    9% decrease in the use of any medications for pain relief

    14% decrease in the risk of newborns being admitted to a special care nursery

    34% decrease in the risk of being dissatisfied with the birth experience*

Dads and Partners

The birth of your child is an experience that belongs to you and your family. A true doula will always encourage fathers and partners to take part in the birth to the extent they that feel comfortable and happy. If they want to be right there holding mama's hand, we will encourage them. A doula can suggest positions, comfort measures and other techniques for fathers and partners. This will ensure that mom is getting the care she needs and other family members can play an active and informed role.
